Smart Fridge

Usability Evaluation | User Research | Prototyping

This project is a team assignment of a human system engineering relating course, which is called Methods and Tools Human System Engineering. In this case study, we're doing research on the refrigerator. Are there potential issues that refrigerators have? How could the refrigerator be improved? Through doing heuristic evaluation and usability tests, the issues would be found and solved.

Braking down


the problem statement


and goals

Refrigerators are a part of our life. Everyone has one at home. And we use fridges everyday. Sometimes, we would find the design of fridges does not fit our usage habits. For example, the food in the fridge would expire someday. However, users may not remember the expiration date of every food in the fridge. Hence, through this case study, we’re doing research on the refrigerator and trying to find the potential usability issues of fridges and solve them.

Research process

Executive Summary

Usability testing is a methodology to test the quality of the usability of a product as well as the user experience of a real life user. For this particular project we decided to create a usability test plan and conduct an imaginary usability test, as it is not feasible to conduct a real-life usability test due to the incredibly expensive cost in developing the prototype of our product. The prototype in which we will be conducting the usability test is designed based on recommendations of improvement that we derived from our previous assignments such as heuristic evaluations and task analysis that we conducted with our users.

In the usability test, we would like to ask participants to complete some tasks about the feature of our improvement on the fridge’s touchscreen interface. Each task would cost participants around 5 (five) minutes. Before and after completing the task, participants are required to answer some questions about how they use their fridge, what they think about the new fridge’s touchscreen interface, and if there’s something that needs to be improved. The usability task would cost participants 30 (thirty) minutes.

Objectives

  • Gain overall ideas of how users feel about the redesigned interface.
  • Collect critical feedback.
  • Find out what is still lacking to be improved.

Target Audience

  • Individuals who have a fridge at home.
  • Individuals who are not satisfied with their current fridge.
  • People who often have rotten food in the fridge.

Methodology

For this particular usability test, we would conduct in-person testing sessions with our users. In order to reduce potential bias or confounding aspects that may affect the result of the test, the testing environment as well as the instruction that is given need to be exactly the same for each participant. Additionally, during the test, the facilitator of the test is not allowed to help the users even when users are frustrated. After a given time - when the participant is still not successful, then the tester is allowed to show the users how to do it.

The participants are required to answer some pre-test questions before they perform the usability test, as well as a post-test questionnaire following the session. As the testers, we will record the steps that each participant took to complete each task. We will also record their “think-out-loud” process to identify users’ frustrations.

Based on the result of the usability test, we will be able to collect the data, analyze and figure out the possible “trends” in which we can take into consideration for improvement opportunities.

Metrics

To measure the success of redesigning the touchscreen interface, metrics we choose to track include performance of the interface, initial performance, error-related issues, and user satisfaction.

  • Initial performance: How long does each task cost testers? We would record how much time testers spend on each task.
  • Error-related issues: The error rates of our design. By observation, we would count how many error-related issues participants encountered.
  • User satisfaction: What do testers think of our design? After tasks are completed, users would be required to score the design.

Pre-test Questionnaire

  1. Have you ever had or used a smart refrigerator before? If yes, what is the brand and tell me your experience?
  2. What are the items in your fridge that you often cannot finish and end up throwing away?
  3. How often do you rearrange items in your refrigerator or clean it?
  4. What are the features from your own refrigerator that you wish to be improved?
  5. If you are buying a new refrigerator, what is your number one concern or priority? What features do you look for?

Tasks

  1. Add a gallon of whole milk to the fridge grocery list manually.
  2. Add a box of rib eyes to the fridge grocery list by scanning the barcode
  3. Check the expiration date of the milk and set the notify date 1 week before
  4. Delete the rib eyes from the fridge grocery list

Post-test Questionnaire

  1. What do you think that can be improved from the smartfridge interface?
  2. From scale 1 to 5 (1 being the hardest and 5 being the easiest), please provide rating for task #1
  3. From scale 1 to 5 (1 being the hardest and 5 being the easiest), please provide rating for task #2
  4. From scale 1 to 5 (1 being the hardest and 5 being the easiest), please provide rating for task #3
  5. From scale 1 to 5 (1 being the hardest and 5 being the easiest), please provide a rating for task #4
  6. If this product comes live, would you purchase it?

Problems

After the usability test, we found the following issues:
  • Forget food expiration date
  • Forget to close the door
  • Unable to readjust fridge divider resulting in stacked food
  • Not spacious enough

Solutions

Addressing user issues:
  • Barcode scanner to input food expiration date: By scanning the barcode, the smart fridge could memorize the expiration date for users.
  • Food expiration date reminder: The fridge would remind users that the food’s expiration date is close. Users could set the time of reminder, like a week before expiration date. Then, the system would remind users.
  • Door timely alarm: Users sometimes would forget to close their fridge’s doors. Hence, the door alarm could remind users to close doors well without wasting electricity.
Enhancing user experience:
  • Music player: The system could also play music when connected to the internet. Some users used to cook with music or radio.
  • Voice Assistant: The voice assistant could help users set up the food expiration date reminder, the temperature, the alarm etc.
  • Recipe recommendation: Through knowing what’s in the fridge, the system could search on the internet and recommend users to cook some dishes from the ingredients in the fridge.

What I learnt

  • This was my first time going through the whole process of user experience research. It took us the whole semester, which is longer than I thought. And every part of the research is critical. None of them could be ignored.
  • Don’t set the expectation of the result. Sometimes, I put my subject feeling and expectation on the research result. Hence, my teammates remind me of getting rid of those expectations.
  • According to the previous point, teammates are crucial in the research process. Diverse opinions and perspectives are the key to stimulate better ideas to solve problems.
