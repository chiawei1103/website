Garmin Blog

Deisgn Research | User Research | UI Redesign

This project is an assignment of a visual communication relating course, which is called Principle of Visual Communication. I’ve done this project on my own. In this case study, I am going to talk about one of Garmin’s pages. Since I like Garmin a lot, I chose to do the redesign of Garmin's Blog, which is not as popular as his other websites, such as Garmin Connect, Garmin Technology, and Garmin Express.

Challenge!

Garmin is a well-known sports portable equipment and GPS company. And it has different pages for different purposes. Like Garmin connect is for creating a fitness community. Garmin Express is for users to update maps and software on the devices. However, the Garmin Blog is not as popular as other pages. Hence, in this project, I am trying to do the research, try to find the problems of the website, and improve the website to reach more people, being more user-friendly.

About the company

Garmin is a multinational technology company founded by Gary Burrell and Min Kao in Kansas, United States. Global Positioning System (GPS) technology for automotive, aviation, marine, outdoor, and sports activities is the specialized field of the company. Developed to this day, Garmin not only has portable products incorporated electronic maps and navigational charts, but also communications devices combined cellular and GPS technology.

Garmin speaks languages about outdoor, safety, and health. Those related words and phrases are expressed in the pictures and the articles they publicized. We could also know that the value of Garmin is sustainability by viewing their website, blog, and social media.

Heuristic Evaluation

While doing the heuristic evaluation on the website, I found that most of the problems on the website are related to visibility of system status, which is about navigation and pagination on the website.

1.

  • Issue: There are several tabs on the top of the page. When entering the page, users don’t know which tabs they’re reading. This navigation issue may confuse users about how to find the category they want to know about.
  • Rating: Intermediate

2.

  • Issue: While using the searching bar on the top, the site doesn’t tell users whether it finished searching or not. It might take users some more time to see if the site finished searching. And the research results sometimes are not related to the query.
  • Rating: Critical

3.

  • Issue: When not finding the article users want, they need to click on “show more” and find the article from all articles, which seems to be endless. Users may have no patience for keeping doing the same thing to find the article. They may just quit.
  • Rating: Intermediate

Solutions

1.   Information architecture

When it comes to searching things, categorizing and sorting play crucial roles. Some users use categories to find what they want, so categorizing related things together could make the finding flow easier. Some users use a search engine to get what they want, then letting users decide how to sort the searching result could help them get what they need quicker. Hence, the combination of categorizing and sorting is necessary for searching. In the revision, users could either select the category they want or type in the search bar.

2.   Pagination

The number and pages of results are one of the things users care about. Users do not have patience and time for clicking “show more” again and again. Users want to know how many they get and how many they left. By knowing that, users could decide whether to keep searching in this category or type in the search bar directly.

3.   Bonus feature

  • Sorted by: users could select the way results are sorted by, which the original version doesn’t have. Users need to know what the results are sorted by to decide whether they need to go to the next page.
  • Titles and tags: In order to differentiate the display of titles and tags, all tags add a gray background. Reducing font similarity could help users distinguish the hierarchy of text.
  • Carousel: The top big picture in the original website is just a picture, which could be made good use of. It could be a space to showcase the latest articles and recommend readers what the trend is recently. So, it becomes a slider for latest articles with their tags, titles, and dates.

What I learnt

  • Searching engines is a big issue. Many small websites use plugins as their searching engine. However, the more critical thing is to categorize their items. Products need to be categorized. Articles need either.
  • To design the layout of every article’s card is more difficult than I thought. In the limited space, showing all information is not possible. We need to do the research on whether the information is important enough to show on cards.
  • Even a big scale company like Garmin, its website is not perfect. There are always shortages to get improved on every system.
